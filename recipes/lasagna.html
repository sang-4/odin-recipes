Lasagna

Description

Lasagna is a type of pasta that is made by layering sheets of pasta with a filling between them. The filling is usually a mixture of meat, vegetables, and cheese. The pasta sheets are often made from egg, flour, and water. Lasagna is a popular dish in many countries, including Italy, the United States, and Canada.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 (28 ounce) can crushed tomatoes
  • 1 (6 ounce) can tomato paste
  • 2 tablespoons white sugar
  • 1 tablespoon dried basil leaves
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano leaves
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1 (16 ounce) package lasagna noodles
  • 16 ounces ricotta cheese
  • 1 egg
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/2 cup mozzarella cheese, shredded

Steps/Procedure

  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C). In a large skillet, cook the ground beef and onion over medium heat until no longer pink; drain. Stir in the tomatoes, tomato paste, sugar, basil, oregano, salt, and pepper. Simmer, uncovered, for 1 hour.
  2. Spread 1 cup of meat sauce in the bottom of a 9x13 inch baking dish. Cover the bottom with a layer of 3 uncooked lasagna noodles. Spread with half of the ricotta cheese mixture, then half of the mozzarella cheese. Repeat layers, and top with remaining noodles and meat sauce. Cover with aluminum foil.
  3. Bake in preheated oven for 25 minutes. Remove foil, and continue baking for 25 to 30 minutes, until noodles are tender.
